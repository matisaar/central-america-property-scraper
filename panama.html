🏛️ Why Panama?

Investment Overview

Panama is the most investor-friendly country in Central America. USD economy eliminates currency risk, no foreign income tax, the world's best retirement visa (Pensionado), and a modern banking hub. The Panama Canal generates stable government revenue, and the country has Latin America's fastest-growing economy.

🏠 Ownership: Full foreign ownership (titled land)
💰 Property Tax: 0% first 3 yrs (new builds)
📋 Closing Costs: 5–8%
💵 Currency: USD (no FX risk)
Biggest advantage: Panama uses the US Dollar. Your rental income stays in USD — zero currency conversion risk. Plus, no tax on income earned outside Panama.
Tax holiday: New construction properties get a property tax exemption for the first 3 years, then phase in at reduced rates. This can save $3,000–10,000+ over the first decade.

💡 Buying Process

Step-by-Step for Canadians

  1. Find a property — work with a licensed agent (look for ACOBIR members)
  2. Verify title — Panama has a titled land system (Registro Público). Hire an attorney to check for liens, ROP status, and titled vs. rights of possession land
  3. Promise to Purchase — sign preliminary agreement with 10% deposit in escrow
  4. Corporation setup — most buyers create a Panamanian S.A. (Sociedad Anónima) for anonymity and liability protection
  5. Close with a notary — pay transfer tax (2%), notary fees, attorney fees
  6. Register ownership — title registered at the Public Registry
Critical: Panama has two types of land — titled (titled) and ROP (Right of Possession). Only buy titled land unless you're very experienced. ROP land is cheaper but carries ownership risk and can be harder to resell.
