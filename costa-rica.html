🏛️ Why Costa Rica?

Investment Overview

Costa Rica offers full foreign property ownership with the same rights as citizens (except within maritime zones). The country has a stable democracy (70+ years), growing tourism (3M+ visitors/yr), and a strong expat community.

🏠 Ownership: Full foreign ownership
💰 Property Tax: 0.25% annually
📋 Closing Costs: 3.5–4.5%
💵 Currency: CRC (USD widely accepted)
Key advantage: Costa Rica has no restrictions on foreign property ownership. You get the same title as a citizen. USD is accepted almost everywhere in tourist areas.
Watch out: Maritime Zone Law — properties within 200m of the high-tide line on both coasts are government-owned. You can only lease (not buy) these concession properties. Always verify title with a local attorney.

💡 Buying Process

Step-by-Step for Canadians

  1. Find a property — work with a licensed agent who knows the local market
  2. Due diligence — hire a Costa Rican attorney to verify title at the National Registry
  3. Make an offer — typically 10% earnest money deposit
  4. Set up a Costa Rican corporation — most foreigners buy through an S.A. (sociedad anónima) for liability protection
  5. Close at a notary — transfer deed, pay transfer tax (1.5%), legal fees (1–2%), stamps
  6. Register the title — attorney registers at National Registry
Critical: Always get an independent title search. Some coastal properties are in the maritime zone (200m from high tide) and can only be leased, not owned. A good attorney will catch this.
