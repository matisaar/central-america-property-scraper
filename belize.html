🏛️ Why Belize?

Investment Overview

Belize is the only English-speaking country in Central America, making it the easiest for Canadian investors. It has the world's 2nd largest barrier reef, a growing tourism sector, and the QRP (Qualified Retired Persons) program that lets you live tax-free. The BZD is pegged 2:1 to USD, providing currency stability.

🏠 Ownership: Full foreign ownership allowed
💰 Property Tax: 1–1.5% annually
📋 Closing Costs: 8–12%
💵 Currency: BZD (pegged 2:1 to USD)
English advantage: All legal documents, contracts, and government proceedings are in English. No translator needed. Common law legal system (like Canada), so property law concepts are familiar.
Watch out: Closing costs in Belize are the highest in Central America (8–12%). The 5% stamp duty is the biggest component. Factor this into your ROI calculations.

🛂 QRP — Qualified Retired Persons Program

The Best Tax-Free Retirement Program in the Caribbean

Belize's QRP program is designed for retirees (45+) who want to live in Belize without paying local taxes. It's one of the most generous programs in the world.

BenefitDetails
Age requirement45 years or older
Income requirement$2,000 USD/mo from pension, annuity, or investments
Tax on foreign income$0 — completely tax-free
Import dutyDuty-free import of personal belongings, car, boat, airplane
Property ownershipFull rights, same as citizens
Work in BelizeCannot work for a Belizean company (can work remotely)
Processing time2–3 months
Key benefit: Under QRP, all income from outside Belize is completely tax-free. No income tax, no capital gains tax, no estate tax on your foreign income and assets.

💡 Buying Process

Step-by-Step for Canadians

  1. Find property — work with a local Belizean real estate agent
  2. Attorney engagement — hire a Belizean attorney (required) to do title search at the Lands Department
  3. Offer & deposit — typically 10% earnest money with an Agreement of Sale
  4. Title search — attorney verifies clear title, no liens, correct survey
  5. Transfer & stamp duty — pay 5% stamp duty + 2–3% attorney fees + misc costs
  6. Registration — deed recorded at the Lands Department
Title caution: Some properties in Belize (especially on islands) have unclear titles dating back to colonial land grants. Always get a thorough title search from an independent attorney — not the seller's attorney.
